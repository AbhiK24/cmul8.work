## CRITICAL: Task-Driven Simulation Design

This simulation is TASK-DRIVEN, not open-ended. The candidate must:
1. Complete specific tasks within 45 minutes
2. Extract information from agents to complete each task
3. Navigate relationships to unlock information

**Each task should require information from 1-3 agents.** The candidate cannot complete tasks without talking to the right people and asking the right questions.

## CRITICAL: Behavioral Trait Coverage (Pymetrics Framework)

Your simulation MUST be designed to probe ALL 9 behavioral traits below. Each task, agent interaction, and scenario element should create opportunities to observe these traits.

### 1. ATTENTION
Signal capture through:
- **Time-to-first-action**: How quickly does candidate engage with urgent vs non-urgent threads?
- **Distraction filtering**: Include decoy emails/messages that are interesting but low-priority
- **Sustained focus**: Tasks requiring extended engagement without switching

Design requirement: Include at least one LOW-PRIORITY but INTERESTING distraction thread. Track which threads candidate opens first and how long they engage with each.

### 2. DECISION MAKING
Signal capture through:
- **Evidence-seeking**: Does candidate ask clarifying questions before deciding?
- **Belief updating**: When new info contradicts initial assumptions, do they adapt?
- **Escalation judgment**: Do they know when to make a call vs when to escalate?

Design requirement: Include a task where initial information is incomplete or misleading. One agent should have contradicting info that requires belief updating.

### 3. EFFORT
Signal capture through:
- **Selective effort**: Do they invest more effort in high-impact tasks?
- **Reward sensitivity**: How do they respond to positive feedback mid-simulation?
- **Consistency**: Is quality maintained across all tasks or does it drop for later ones?

Design requirement: Tasks should vary in importance/visibility. Include at least one "quick win" task and one "high effort, high impact" task.

### 4. EMOTION (Emotional Intelligence)
Signal capture through:
- **Subtext reading**: Can they detect when an agent is frustrated, anxious, or hiding something?
- **Register calibration**: Do they adjust tone for different stakeholders (peer vs executive vs report)?
- **Client empathy**: Can they acknowledge feelings while still addressing business needs?

Design requirement: One agent should have an emotional undertone (frustrated, anxious, passive-aggressive). Include both senior and junior stakeholders requiring different communication registers.

### 5. FAIRNESS
Signal capture through:
- **Process fairness**: Do they consider how decisions affect different team members?
- **Resource allocation**: When resources are limited, how do they distribute them?

Design requirement: Include a scenario with competing stakeholder needs or limited resources where the "fair" answer isn't obvious.

### 6. FOCUS (Adaptability)
Signal capture through:
- **Context switching**: How well do they handle interruptions (stress injects)?
- **Adaptability**: When plans change mid-task, do they pivot gracefully?

Design requirement: Stress injects should require immediate context switches. At least one inject should invalidate or significantly change a previous task.

### 7. GENEROSITY (Collaboration)
Signal capture through:
- **Trust extension**: Do they give agents the benefit of the doubt?
- **Information sharing**: Do they proactively share useful info with agents who might need it?

Design requirement: Create opportunities where candidate has info that would help an agent. Include an agent who initially seems unhelpful but becomes valuable with trust.

### 8. LEARNING
Signal capture through:
- **Error acknowledgment**: If they make a mistake, do they own it?
- **Pattern detection**: Can they connect dots across different conversations?
- **Recovery quality**: After setbacks, do they recover well or spiral?

Design requirement: Include scattered information across agents that forms a pattern when connected. One task should build on another, testing pattern recognition.

### 9. RISK TOLERANCE
Signal capture through:
- **Ambiguity commitment**: Can they make decisions with incomplete information?
- **Downside sensitivity**: Do they over-focus on what could go wrong?
- **Social risk**: Will they push back on senior stakeholders when warranted?

Design requirement: Include a decision that must be made without full information. Include a scenario where the "safe" answer isn't necessarily the "right" answer. One senior agent should propose something questionable.

## Your Task
Generate a complete simulation environment:

1. **Company Details**: Use EXACTLY the company name "{org_name}". Base the company description on the context provided above.

2. **Scenario Tension**: A realistic workplace challenge that creates urgency and context for the tasks.

3. **Task Queue (3-5 tasks)**: Each task must have:
   - Clear deliverable (decision, document, recommendation)
   - `required_info`: List of agents who hold pieces needed to complete it
   - `completion_type`: How candidate proves completion:
     - "chat_validation": Must show/explain work to validating_agent
     - "artifact_edit": Must update a section of the artifact
     - "deliverable": Must describe/present solution in chat
   - `validating_agent_id`: Who signs off on completion
   - `deliverable_prompt`: What the agent will ask for to validate

4. **Agent Cast (4-5 agents)**: Each agent must have:
   - Unique personality and relationship to candidate
   - `task_knowledge`: List of tasks they have info for, with:
     - What info they hold
     - Condition for sharing ("asked directly", "relationship > 0.6", "only after they share X")
   - `hidden_information`: Info they won't share easily
   - **Autonomy fields** (agents can initiate contact!):
     - `proactivity`: 0.0-1.0, how likely to reach out unprompted (managers: 0.5-0.7, peers: 0.2-0.4)
     - `current_concern`: What's on their mind that they might bring up (e.g., "worried about the deadline")
     - `will_initiate_about`: List of topics they might message the candidate about first
   - **Availability schedule** (agents have meetings/calls):
     - `availability_schedule`: Array of {at_seconds, state, duration_seconds, reason}
     - States: "active", "busy", "in_meeting", "away", "dnd"
     - Example: Manager is in meetings at 5min and 20min marks
     - Creates realistic "waiting for X to be free" situations
   - **Help requests** (agents can ask candidate for help!):
     - `help_requests`: Array of {trigger_seconds, topic, message, context, priority}
     - Example: At 10 minutes, Sarah asks for help reviewing her client deck
     - Flips the dynamic - candidate must decide whether to help or focus on own tasks

5. **Background Chatter (2-3 conversations)**: Pre-existing conversations between agents that they may reference:
   - Agent A and B discussed topic X
   - Creates realism ("I was just talking to Sarah about this...")
   - Can reveal info or create complications

6. **Initial Inbox**: 3-4 email threads that relate to tasks

7. **Stress Injects**: 3-4 timed interruptions at minutes 5, 15, 25, 35

8. **Artifact**: A role-appropriate work document with editable sections linked to tasks:
   - PM: PRD with sections (Problem, Solution, Requirements, Timeline, Risks)
   - Analyst: Analysis report (Executive Summary, Data Findings, Recommendations)
   - Eng Manager: Technical spec (Overview, Architecture, Implementation, Timeline)
   - Sales: Proposal (Client Needs, Solution, Pricing, Next Steps)
   - Ops Lead: Process document (Current State, Issues, Proposed Changes, Rollout)

   Each section should have:
   - `section_id`: Unique identifier
   - `title`: Section heading
   - `content`: Pre-filled content (some correct, some needing fixes based on info from agents)
   - `linked_task_id`: Which task requires editing this section (optional)
   - `editable`: true/false

   IMPORTANT: Some sections should contain INCORRECT or INCOMPLETE information that the candidate needs to fix by gathering info from agents. This creates the core gameplay loop.

9. **End Conditions (Win/Fail States)**: Define clear success and failure conditions that create stakes:

   **Fail Conditions** (at least 2-3):
   - `relationship_threshold`: Relationship with critical agent drops below threshold (e.g., 0.15)
   - `agent_escalation`: A "difficult" agent escalates to their boss after poor handling
   - `time_limit`: Critical task not completed before deadline (link to inject timing)

   **Win Conditions**:
   - `task_completion`: All required tasks completed with good relationships (>0.4)

   Each end condition should have:
   - `type`: "win" or "fail"
   - `trigger`: What triggers the condition
   - `description`: Message shown to candidate when triggered
   - `threshold`: For relationship-based triggers (0.0-1.0)
   - `agent_id`: For agent-specific triggers
   - `trigger_seconds`: For time_limit - when to check (should align with inject_schedule)
   - `required_task_id`: For time_limit - which task must be done by then

   **Example time_limit condition:**
   ```json
   {
     "type": "fail",
     "trigger": "time_limit",
     "description": "The client meeting started and you haven't prepared the brief! Your manager is furious.",
     "trigger_seconds": 900,
     "required_task_id": "task_prepare_brief"
   }
   ```

   IMPORTANT: End conditions create stakes and engagement. A "difficult" agent should have potential to cause a FAIL if mishandled. Time-based conditions create urgency and force prioritization. This makes the simulation feel consequential.
