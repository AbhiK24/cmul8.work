## Assessment Framework (Pymetrics Behavioral Traits)
Evaluate the candidate on these 9 behavioral dimensions (score each 1-10):

1. **ATTENTION**: How well did they filter distractions and sustain focus?
   - Time-to-first-action: How quickly did they engage with urgent threads?
   - Distraction filtering: Did they ignore low-priority but interesting threads?
   - Sustained focus: Did they complete threads before jumping to new ones?
   - Consider: Thread open order, time spent on each, engagement with decoy content

2. **DECISION MAKING**: How well did they gather evidence and update beliefs?
   - Evidence-seeking: Did they ask clarifying questions before deciding?
   - Belief updating: When contradicting info emerged, did they adapt?
   - Escalation judgment: Did they know when to decide vs when to escalate?
   - Consider: Questions asked, how they handled conflicting information, decision quality

3. **EFFORT**: Did they allocate effort strategically?
   - Selective effort: More investment in high-impact tasks?
   - Reward sensitivity: How did they respond to positive feedback?
   - Consistency: Was quality maintained across all tasks?
   - Consider: Message depth per task importance, quality variance across tasks

4. **EMOTION (Emotional Intelligence)**: Did they read and respond to emotional cues?
   - Subtext reading: Did they detect frustration, anxiety, or hidden concerns?
   - Register calibration: Did they adjust tone for different stakeholders?
   - Client empathy: Did they acknowledge feelings while addressing business needs?
   - Consider: Tone adaptation, response to emotional agents, stakeholder sensitivity

5. **FAIRNESS**: Did they consider equitable outcomes?
   - Process fairness: Did they consider how decisions affect different team members?
   - Resource allocation: When resources were limited, how did they distribute?
   - Consider: Trade-off reasoning, stakeholder consideration, balanced decisions

6. **FOCUS (Adaptability)**: How well did they handle interruptions and change?
   - Context switching: How effectively did they handle stress injects?
   - Adaptability: When plans changed, did they pivot gracefully?
   - Consider: Inject response times, pivot behavior, recovery speed

7. **GENEROSITY (Collaboration)**: Did they build trust and share proactively?
   - Trust extension: Did they give agents the benefit of the doubt?
   - Information sharing: Did they proactively share useful info with agents?
   - Consider: Relationship score improvements, proactive communication, trust-building

8. **LEARNING**: Did they recognize patterns and recover from setbacks?
   - Error acknowledgment: If they made mistakes, did they own them?
   - Pattern detection: Did they connect information across conversations?
   - Recovery quality: After setbacks, did they recover well?
   - Consider: Pattern recognition, recovery behavior, knowledge synthesis

9. **RISK TOLERANCE**: How did they handle ambiguity and social risk?
   - Ambiguity commitment: Could they decide with incomplete information?
   - Downside sensitivity: Did they over-focus on what could go wrong?
   - Social risk: Did they push back on senior stakeholders when warranted?
   - Consider: Decision confidence, risk framing, willingness to challenge
